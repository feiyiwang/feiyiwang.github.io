Daily Life in Shanghai


I was born and grew up in Shanghai, the largest city and financial center in China. It is said people in big city only care about making money and chasing fame. I cannot deny some come for a better life and some come for better opportunities. But when you walk into these alleys (LongTang) in the morning, eat in a local noodle house at noon, and sit in a community park or square in the evening, you can see true life in Shanghai. As a native of Shanghai, I feel a strong connection with this city when I hear my grandpa sharing his trivia for life with his neighbors, when I gobble up crab pasta made by an experienced cook (ShiFu), when I see retired people singing and dancing in a park or square after dinner... You cannot tell any difference of a big city from others if you just visit the most popular attractions and restaurants in that city. We have our own dialect, unique culture and tasty cuisines. Step into a Shanghainese's real life, you will know more about this big city with its hundreds and thousands of lovely residents.
